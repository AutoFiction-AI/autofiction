# Ethics

## Position

We release AutoFiction as a research artifact to promote transparency and reproducibility in long-form AI writing research.

The project does not encourage deceptive publishing or hiding authorship. We maintain that novels generated in whole or in part through this pipeline should be disclosed as such when they are publicly distributed. Publishing AI-generated work without disclosure is unethical and deceptive to readers, editors, publishers, and reviewers.

All novels on [autofiction.ai](https://autofiction.ai) are labeled as AI-generated. 

## Disclosure Expectations

When distributing work created with this system, you should clearly disclose if: 
- AI produced most of the text
- humans played any role in editing or writing the text
- there are intermediate artifacts that show how the text was created
The exact wording depends on the venue of the work.

## Why We Are Releasing This

This repository supports the evaluation and reproducibility of research on AI-generated fiction, as well as conversations about the failure modes of AI-generated fiction. We are not releasing it as a tool for people to distribute undisclosed AI-generated books. 

## Commercialization And Outputs

The Apache 2.0 software license attached to this repository permits broad reuse including commercial reuse. That being said, we still do not support the commercialization of generated outputs without explicit disclosure and compliance with platform or venue policies. You are responsible for licensing any generated outputs separately following venue disclosure requirements and any applicable laws. 

The models we use in this pipeline were trained on datasets that include creative work created by humans. The implications of such method is controversial, and we take a neutral position in this project.

Generated books and the artifact corpus are published under [CC BY-NC 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/), separate from the Apache 2.0 license on this codebase.

## Unacceptable Uses

Unacceptable uses include:
- submitting AI-generated books to publishers without proper disclosure
- misrepresenting provenance or editing history to hide AI usage
- impersonating living authors or misleading readers about sources. 
- using generated content in ways that violate privacy, copyright, or other legal rights

## Keeping Records

Where possible, please keep and publish records of generation workflow artifacts, including prompts, reviews, revision reports, etc. We acknowledge that detailed records cannot fully prevent misuse, but it is somewhat better than asking readers to blindly trust unsupported claims.

## Impact On Human Authors

AI-generated fiction poses a threat to the livelihood of writers and the integrity of the creative economy. Automated systems that produce novels at low cost could depress markets that human authors depend on. In fact, new book releases [tripled between 2022 and 2025](https://www.nber.org/papers/w34777), with new entrants producing sloppy work, which makes the publishing industry vulnerable to the increase in undisclosed AI-generated content. 

This project does not resolve those concerns, and transparency alone is not a sufficient answer. However, the debate would benefit from transparent systems, rather than closed systems. 

This document states norms and expectations, not legal advice. Responsible use depends on downstream users.
