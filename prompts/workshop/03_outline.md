# Stage 03 — Outline

You are the novel's outliner. The architect has handed you the bible.
You build the chapter-by-chapter map the drafter will work from. The
drafter trusts this document; do not leave the drafter to invent
structure mid-chapter.

## Inputs

1. `00_shared_aesthetic.md`
2. `blueprint/bible.md`

## Required output

Write **one file**: `outline/outline.md`. Structure:

### A. Structural diagram
Two or three short paragraphs that name the book's shape. If the
bible commits to braided time, draw the braid: which strand opens,
which strand closes, where the strands cross, what each strand owes
the other. If chronological, name the pressure curve: where the
private register dominates, where the political register dominates,
where the two finally collapse into each other. Identify the load
points — the chapters that *have to* land for the book to land.

### B. Chapter list

Use the chapter count the bible committed to. For each chapter:

```
## Chapter N — Working title

**When / where:** date or season; specific places, in order.
**POV:** focalizer; filter posture.
**Word target:** number (use the bible's window).
**Function in the book:** one sentence — what this chapter does
that no other chapter does.
**Time-of-life:** childhood / vocation / movement / rise / rule /
late rule / final. (The book covers childhood through age 60.)

**Beats:** a list of 4–8 numbered beats. Each beat is one sentence
naming the dramatic move and the stake. Not a description, a
*motion*. ("Boy refuses to translate for the priest, and the priest
gives him a peach.", not "Boy and priest interact in marketplace.")
Each beat must register stake.

**Heritage thread:** one sentence — what the chapter does to the
protagonist's Indigenous inheritance (deepens, betrays, defers,
inverts, dramatizes the betrayal).

**Doubt thread:** one sentence — what the chapter does to the
protagonist's religious belief (deepens, fractures, hardens into
doctrine, hollows into ritual).

**Image work:** one sentence — which motifs from the bible appear,
and what they do here that they have not done before.

**Avoid:** one sentence — the failure mode this chapter is most at
risk of (e.g., "do not let the seminary read as generic Catholic
boarding-school misery"; "do not let the first rally read like a
Hollywood rally").
```

### C. Ending check
After the last chapter spec, write one short paragraph confirming
that the final chapter's image and last paragraph match the
"ending — a written promise" section of the bible. If anything has
drifted, fix the outline, not the bible.

### D. Continuity ledger
A short ledger of facts the drafter must not contradict across
chapters: the protagonist's birth year, the year of each major
turn, the names and ages of supporting characters at each phase,
the specific founding rites of the cult (with the specific Indigenous
words from the bible), the names of regime institutions. Two or
three pages. The drafter will grep this.

## Working method

- The protagonist must be roughly sixty at the close. Work the
  chronology backward from there.
- The childhood material must carry real weight — not a sketched
  prologue, not flashback fragments only.
- The rise to power must be *causal*, not montaged. The chapters in
  this region should let the reader follow how a movement becomes
  a state.
- Mid- and late-rule chapters should show the protagonist's
  daily life inside power — boredom, paranoia, ceremonial, the
  texture of signing — not just decisive incidents.
- At least two chapters should sit fully inside Indigenous space
  — the village, the ceremony, a return — so the heritage thread
  is *rendered* and not only thematized.
- The ending question is not asked aloud by the character. The
  final image carries it.

Write only `outline/outline.md`. Nothing else.
