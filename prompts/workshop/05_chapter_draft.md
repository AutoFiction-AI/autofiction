# Stage 05 — Chapter Draft

You are drafting **Chapter {{CHAPTER_NUMBER}} — {{CHAPTER_TITLE}}** of
this novel. This is the first full draft of this chapter. A later
stage will revise. Do the chapter the outline asked you to do; do not
write a different chapter.

## Inputs (read in this order, every time)

1. `00_shared_aesthetic.md` — binding aesthetic.
2. `voice/style_guide.md` — binding voice and language rules.
3. `blueprint/bible.md` — the world. Especially: dramatis personae,
   continuity ledger entries, motif inventory.
4. `outline/outline.md` — re-read the spec for this chapter and the
   chapter immediately before it. Note the continuity ledger.
5. Every previously drafted chapter in `drafts/v1/` whose number is
   less than this one — read them. You must not contradict them in
   fact, image, or voice. The narrator should remember.

## Required output

Write **one file**: `drafts/v1/chapter_{{CHAPTER_NUMBER_PADDED}}.md`.

Format:

- First non-empty line: `# Chapter {{CHAPTER_NUMBER}} — {{CHAPTER_TITLE}}`
- Then the chapter, in prose. Scene breaks: a single blank line, then a
  centered `***` on its own line, then a blank line.
- No headers other than the chapter heading. No author notes. No
  process leakage.
- Word target: the window the outline named for this chapter. Land
  inside the window. Going long is not a virtue.

## How to draft

This is fiction-writing, not summary. The outline's beats are the
*motions* of the chapter — render each as scene, not as exposition.
A beat that says "Boy refuses to translate for the priest" becomes
several minutes of present, embodied action in the marketplace, in
the boy's body, in the priest's body, in the indifferent vendors'
periphery. The outline does not write the scene; you do.

Hold the load:

- **Heritage thread.** The chapter's heritage beat is rendered, not
  thematized. Do not have the narrator (or worse, the protagonist)
  comment on what he is losing or keeping. Show the act, the
  syntactic switch, the refusal, the shame, the recovery. The reader
  feels it because the scene carries it.
- **Doubt thread.** Same discipline. The protagonist's belief or
  unbelief shows in *what he does when nobody is watching*, in *how
  he says a prayer he has said a thousand times*, in *what he reaches
  for in panic*. The doubt thread is never named on the page as a
  thread.
- **Stake.** Every scene must register the possibility of change in
  the room: a relational shift, a piece of information moving between
  characters, a body cost, a leverage move. Stretches of ordinary
  dialogue are allowed (and welcome) when they *do* texture work,
  not as filler.
- **Sentence-level discipline.** Re-read clauses 4 and 9 of the
  shared aesthetic before drafting. Reread the style guide's
  "forbidden moves" before you write the closing paragraph of the
  chapter. The chapter cannot end on a tidy beat of recognition; it
  cannot end on a personified abstraction; it cannot end on a
  rhythm-perfect capper.
- **Language.** Use the Spanish and the Indigenous words the bible
  committed to, in the way the style guide committed to. No
  translation in prose unless one character is translating for
  another. No italics-as-tourism.
- **Cast voices.** Each speaking character must produce sentences that
  could not have come from any other character in this chapter. If
  two voices flatten into each other, fix it now, not later.

## What the chapter must not do

- Narrate the protagonist's interior in the register of a literary
  reviewer ("he felt, in that moment, the weight of...").
- Use grief, violence, religion, or class as decorative weight on a
  scene that has not earned its weight.
- End on a quiet beat of recognition.
- Recap prior chapters. Let the reader carry the past forward
  themselves. References to prior material are gestures and objects,
  not summaries.
- Use any forbidden vocabulary or motif from the style guide.
- Reproduce text from any dialogue reference samples, prior books in
  the dictator-novel lineage, or any specific real person, place, or
  ceremony.

## Closing

Write the chapter, save it at the required path. Do not write
anything else. Do not produce notes, plans, or a summary in the file.
