# Stage 12 — Final Read

This is the last read before the final polish. The manuscript is in
its fourth revision. You are reading it as a reader would — opening
to chapter 1 cold, going through to the end without stopping. You
are checking three things:

1. **Does the book land?** Specifically: does the closing chapter
   carry the premise's question — *can a lie lived long enough
   become true?* — through image and behavior rather than
   statement? Does the final paragraph rest where the bible's
   promise said it would?
2. **Do the seams show?** Between chapters and within chapters,
   after three editing passes, is there anywhere the manuscript
   reads as edited — repeated information, a character introduced
   twice, a callback that arrives before its setup, a tonal jolt at
   a chapter boundary?
3. **Did anything regress?** Across four drafts, occasionally a
   line revision damages a chapter's voice; occasionally a
   character/theme edit accidentally muddies a scene the
   structural pass had cleaned. Catch these.

## Inputs

1. `00_shared_aesthetic.md`
2. `blueprint/bible.md`
3. `outline/outline.md`
4. `voice/style_guide.md`
5. Every file in `drafts/v4/` in chapter order.

Read the manuscript end to end first, in one sitting if possible.

## Required output

Write **one file**: `reads/v4_final_notes.md`. Structure:

### A. The reader's verdict
One paragraph. The book in one paragraph, as a reader would
describe it after finishing. Not a blurb. The shape of the
experience.

### B. Title decision
The bible offered three candidate titles. After reading the
manuscript, recommend a final title — one of the candidates, or a
new one drawn from the manuscript's own language. One paragraph
of justification.

### C. Ending check
Read the final chapter twice. Then write:
- Whether the closing image lands the premise's question without
  speaking it.
- Whether the last paragraph is the last paragraph (i.e., the
  chapter does not over-run its true ending by a paragraph or
  two — a frequent failure of late drafts).
- Concrete instructions for the final-polish stage: a sentence to
  cut, an image to load more, a final-line replacement if needed.
  Verbatim quotes from the v4 chapter where useful.

### D. Seam-check
Walk through every chapter boundary. For each: any redundancy,
hand-off problem, or tonal jolt. Polish-pass fixes only —
nothing that requires real revision.

### E. Regression flags
Any chapter where v4 has lost something v3 had. Quote the v3
line and the v4 line. Recommend the restoration or a third
formulation.

### F. Per-chapter polish list
Only chapters that still have polishable issues. Each entry:
the chapter number and a short numbered list of last-pass
sentence-level moves — no scene work, no theme work, no
restructure.

### G. Front- and back-matter notes
One short list: epigraph? dedication? chronology page? glossary
of Spanish and Indigenous-language terms? author's note on
sources? Recommend yes/no for each, and where the polish stage
should source the language from inside the manuscript itself if
yes.

### H. Sign-off
One sentence. Either: "Ready for final polish." Or: a clear
statement of what is missing and what additional pass is needed
before final polish (rare; only if a structural or character
problem survived four drafts).

## Voice

The voice of a senior editor at the end of a long project.
Direct, useful, no flourish. Write only the notes file.
